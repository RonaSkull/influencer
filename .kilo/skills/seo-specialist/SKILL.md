## Keyword Research Strategy

### Finding Winners

**Criteria:**
- Search volume: 500-5,000/month (sweet spot)
- Keyword difficulty: <30 (achievable for new content)
- Commercial intent: High (for monetization)
- Topic relevance: Matches site niche

**Tools:**
- Ahrefs (premium, best)
- SEMrush (good alternative)
- Ubersuggest (budget option)
- Google Search Console (free, your own data)
- Google autocomplete (free, reveals popular searches)

### Keyword Types

**Short-tail** (1-2 words)
- Example: "wall art"
- Volume: HIGH
- Difficulty: HIGH
- Use: Difficult to rank, but try for secondary keywords

**Medium-tail** (3-4 words)
- Example: "modern wall art living room"
- Volume: MEDIUM
- Difficulty: MEDIUM
- Use: Sweet spot for new content

**Long-tail** (5+ words)
- Example: "modern farmhouse wall art for living room above couch"
- Volume: LOW
- Difficulty: LOW
- Use: Easy wins, very targeted traffic

**Recommendation:** 70% medium-tail, 30% long-tail for new content

## On-Page SEO Checklist

### Every Post Must Have:

**Title Tag** (50-60 characters)
- Primary keyword at start
- Compelling, clickable
- Example: "Modern LDS Wall Art: 25 Beautiful Prints for Your Home"

**Meta Description** (150-160 characters)
- Secondary keywords
- Benefit-driven
- CTA
- Example: "Discover 25 stunning LDS wall art prints perfect for modern homes. Instant download, print at home. Shop inspirational scripture art now!"

**URL Slug**
- Short, keyword-rich
- Hyphens (not underscores)
- Example: `/modern-lds-wall-art/` (not `/post-12345/`)

**H1 Heading** (one per page)
- Primary keyword included
- Matches (or close to) title tag
- Example: "25 Modern LDS Wall Art Prints for Your Home"

**H2-H6 Subheadings**
- Secondary keywords
- Clear structure
- User-friendly (scannable)

**Image Alt Text**
- Descriptive + keyword
- Every image (accessibility + SEO)
- Example: "Modern LDS scripture wall art print in living room"

**Internal Links**
- 3-5 per post minimum
- Link to related content
- Anchor text includes keywords

**External Links**
- 1-3 authoritative sources
- Builds trust with Google
- Opens in new tab (keeps users on site)

## Technical SEO

### Site Health Checklist

**Core Web Vitals** (Google ranking factor)
- LCP (Largest Contentful Paint): <2.5s
- FID (First Input Delay): <100ms
- CLS (Cumulative Layout Shift): <0.1

**Actions:**
- Optimize images (WebP format, compression)
- Enable caching
- Use CDN (Cloudflare, free tier)
- Minimize JavaScript

**Mobile-Friendliness**
- Responsive design (must-have)
- Touch-friendly buttons
- Readable text (no tiny fonts)
- Test: Google Mobile-Friendly Test

**Site Speed**
- Target: <3 seconds load time
- Tools: GTmetrix, PageSpeed Insights
- Common fixes: Image compression, caching, minify CSS/JS

**HTTPS**
- SSL certificate (security + ranking factor)
- Should already have (check for mixed content warnings)

**XML Sitemap**
- Auto-generated (WordPress plugins do this)
- Submit to Google Search Console
- Update when new content published

**Robots.txt**
- Tells Google what to crawl
- Don't block important pages
- Allow search engines

## Google Algorithm Updates

### Major Updates to Know

**Helpful Content Update (HCU)** - 2022-2023
- Punishes low-quality, AI-generated, or thin content
- **Impact:** We Heart This got hit (Google HCU victim)
- **Recovery:** Improve content quality, add expertise, remove thin content

**Core Updates** - Quarterly
- General algorithm improvements
- Can cause ranking volatility
- **Strategy:** Focus on quality, don't panic

**Spam Updates** - Ongoing
- Target manipulative tactics
- Link schemes, keyword stuffing, cloaking
- **Strategy:** Play it straight (white-hat SEO only)

## Link Building

### White-Hat Strategies

**Internal Linking** (Easiest)
- Link new posts to old posts
- Link old posts to new posts (update existing content)
- Topic clusters (pillar posts + supporting posts)

**Guest Posting** (Medium difficulty)
- Write for other blogs in your niche
- Include link back to your site
- Builds authority + traffic

**Resource Pages** (Easy wins)
- Find "[topic] resources" pages
- Pitch your content for inclusion
- Usually free or low-effort

**Broken Link Building** (Advanced)
- Find broken links on other sites
- Offer your content as replacement
- Helps them + gets you a link

**HARO (Help A Reporter Out)** (Time-consuming but valuable)
- Respond to journalist requests
- Get quoted + link from major publications
- Huge authority boost

### What to Avoid

❌ **Buying links** (Google penalty)
❌ **Link farms** (spammy, worthless)
❌ **Exact-match anchor text** (looks manipulative)
❌ **Footer/sidebar links** (low value)
❌ **Link exchanges** (outdated, risky)
