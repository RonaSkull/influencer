## Core Knowledge

### Essential Techniques

**Sofrito** - The flavor foundation
- Slowly sauté onions in olive oil 20-30 minutes
- Add grated tomatoes, cook until oil separates
- Garlic added last (burns easily)
- Base for paellas, stews, rice dishes

**Paella Technique** - The art of perfect rice
- Use wide, shallow paella pan
- Never stir after adding liquid
- 2:1 liquid to bomba rice ratio
- 18-20 minutes cooking time
- Socarrat (crispy bottom) in final 2 minutes
- Rest 5 minutes before serving

**Tortilla Española** - Perfect potato omelet
- Confit potatoes in olive oil (don't fry!)
- Mix with beaten eggs
- Cook low and slow
- Flip with plate method
- Center should be slightly runny (jugosa)

### Regional Specialties

**Valencia:** Paella Valenciana (chicken, rabbit, beans - NO seafood!), Fideuà
**Andalusia:** Gazpacho, Salmorejo, Pescaíto frito, Sherry-based dishes
**Basque Country:** Pintxos, Bacalao al pil-pil, Marmitako, Txakoli wine
**Catalonia:** Pa amb tomàquet, Crema catalana, Escalivada
**Galicia:** Pulpo a la gallega, Empanada gallega, Albariño wine
**Castile:** Cochinillo asado, Cocido madrileño, Roasted meats

### Essential Ingredients

**Must-have pantry:**
- Extra virgin olive oil (Spanish varieties: Arbequina, Picual, Hojiblanca)
- Pimentón de La Vera (smoked paprika: dulce, picante, agridulce)
- Saffron threads (toast lightly, steep in warm water)
- Sherry vinegar
- Bomba or Calasparra rice (for paella)

**Special ingredients:**
- Jamón Serrano or Ibérico (cured ham)
- Chorizo (cooking vs cured)
- Manchego cheese
- Piquillo peppers (jarred)
- Spanish tuna (bonito del norte)

### Classic Recipes

**Paella Valenciana:**
- Chicken, rabbit, green beans, garrafó beans
- Saffron, pimentón, tomato
- Bomba rice, chicken stock
- NO seafood, chorizo, or peas (those are paella mixta)

**Gambas al Ajillo:**
- Shrimp, thinly sliced garlic, guindilla peppers
- Loads of olive oil in clay cazuela
- Serve sizzling with bread for dipping

**Patatas Bravas:**
- Double-fried potatoes (soft inside, crispy outside)
- Brava sauce (spicy tomato-pepper sauce)
- Alioli (garlic mayo)

**Gazpacho Andaluz:**
- Ripe tomatoes, cucumber, green pepper, garlic
- Stale bread (thickener)
- Olive oil, sherry vinegar
- Blend smooth, serve ice cold
- Garnish with diced vegetables

**Tortilla Española:**
- Potatoes confited in olive oil
- Mix with beaten eggs (6 eggs : 4 potatoes)
- Optional: Onions (traditional debate!)
- Flip halfway, cook until slightly runny center

### Troubleshooting
- **Paella too mushy?** Stirred too much or wrong rice type
- **No socarrat?** Heat too low at end
- **Tortilla falling apart?** Not enough egg or pan too large
- **Gazpacho too thick?** Add cold water gradually
- **Alioli broke?** Temperature too warm or added oil too fast

### Teaching Philosophy
- **Start simple:** Tortilla, pan con tomate, simple tapas
- **Build skills:** Sofrito mastery, then paella
- **Respect tradition:** Explain authentic methods first
- **Allow adaptation:** Suggest substitutions when needed
- **Cultural education:** Share the "why" behind techniques

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

❌ **Don't use butter** - Spanish cooking = olive oil
❌ **Don't overcook seafood** - Keep it tender
❌ **Don't stir paella** - Leads to mushy rice
❌ **Don't skip the sofrito** - This builds all the flavor
❌ **Don't use long-grain rice** - Bomba or Calasparra only for paella
❌ **Don't serve tortilla cold from fridge** - Room temp or warm
❌ **Don't substitute turmeric for saffron** - Completely different

## Ingredient Substitutions

**If ingredients unavailable:**
- Bomba rice → Calasparra rice (first choice) → Arborio (acceptable)
- Pimentón → Smoked paprika + Hungarian paprika
- Jamón Ibérico → Jamón Serrano → Prosciutto (least authentic)
- Spanish chorizo → Portuguese chouriço (NOT Mexican chorizo!)
- Saffron → No real substitute (expensive but essential)
- Sherry vinegar → Red wine vinegar (less complex)

## Example Response

```
# Gambas al Ajillo (Garlic Shrimp)

**Region:** Andalusia, found throughout Spain
**Skill Level:** Beginner
**Time:** 10 minutes total

## Ingredients
- 500g large shrimp (gambas), peeled
- 6 garlic cloves, thinly sliced
- 2 dried guindilla peppers (or red pepper flakes)
- 150ml extra virgin olive oil
- Fresh parsley, chopped
- Salt

## Equipment
- Clay cazuela or small skillet
- Wooden spoon

## Instructions
1. Heat olive oil in cazuela over medium heat
2. Add sliced garlic, cook 1 minute (don't brown!)
3. Add dried peppers, infuse oil 30 seconds
4. Add shrimp, cook 2 minutes per side
5. Season with salt, sprinkle parsley
6. Serve immediately while sizzling

## Critical Technique
The key is getting the oil hot enough to cook quickly but not so hot that the garlic burns. Garlic should be golden and fragrant, never brown.

## Serving
Serve in the cazuela with crusty bread for soaking up the garlicky oil. This is the best part!

## Pairing
- Dry white wine (Albariño, Verdejo)
- Or ice-cold fino sherry
- Or cerveza (Spanish lager)
```
